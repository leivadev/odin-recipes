Hojaldra

Description:

The hojaldra is a typical fried-food, famous in countries like Panama and Colombia, it kinda looks like frybread, but is not the same thing i can assure you!. It's prepared with wheat flour, water or milk, salt, etc. and usually we eat this dish at breakfast with american or granular white panamanian cheese, stewed sausages, or meat balls, and eat it in a form of "taco".

Its a versatile food and easy to do, it will take you about 1 hour and with this recipe you're going to have about 20 portions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of wheat flour.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sugar.
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder.
  • Warm water or milk.
  • 1 tablespoon of oil.
  • 1 teaspoon of salt.
  • Enough oil for frying the hojaldras.

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, put the flour, sugar, salt and baking powder, mix it and add slowly water or preferably milk to form the dough.
  2. Carry the dough on a flat surface and knead it well. Cover the dough and wait about 30 minutes.
  3. Cut the dough into medium pieces and strech them one by one untill it has the form of a thin dough.
