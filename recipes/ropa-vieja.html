Ropa vieja panameña

Description:

This recipe is originally from the caribbean with regional variations like the cuban or the spanish, and panamanians love this meal, it consist about a delectable shredded beef with some vegetables, soaked up with special sauce and it can be eaten with rice in the lunch, or in the dinner with some "patacones" (fried plantain slices)

Making this recipe will take you about an hour to be prepared, and it will be enough for 3-4 people.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg (2.2 lb) of flank steak.
  • 2 medium white onions.
  • tomato paste to your liking.
  • oil of your preference (for this recipe we will use olive oil).
  • 2 bell tomatoes, preferaly the green ones.
  • 4 cilantro (coriander) sheets.
  • 4 garlic cloves.
  • salt and pepper.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the flank steak, take it to a pot with enoguh water and cook it until the meat gets tender.
  2. Take out the meat and wait for the meat to cool down to shred it. Important: Keep the beef stock in the pot, we're gonna use it later.
  3. Cut the onions and peppers in julienne, the garlic in little pieces and the tomatoes in little cubes.
  4. Heat a pan with some oil and put the vegetables already cutted into the pan.
  5. Add tomato paste for the sauce.
  6. Incorporate the shredded beef and the stock in the pan.
  7. Add the cilantro, salt and pepper and cook everything at low heat for 30 minutes till the sauce gets reduced.
  8. When the sauce is almost reduced, turn off the heat and wait some minutes to cool down.
  9. Your ropa vieja is ready! you can eat this with rice, patacones or potatoes. ¡Enjoy!
